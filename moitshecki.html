Speaker: Joel Moitshecki

Title: Symmetry and Related Analysis of Equations for Solute Transport in Soil.

Date: 18 April 2002

Abstract:

Solute transport in soil is represented by the
convection-dispersion equations (CDE). A form of the two-dimensional solute
transport equation is transformed from Cartesian to streamline coordinates.
Symmetry analysis of this equation with a point water source reveals a
5-parameter symmetry group. Exploitation of the rich symmetry properties of
the equation leads to a number of associated reduced partial differential
equations. Using further symmetry reductions and other transformation
techniques, the construction of new solutions for non-radial solute
transport on a background of radial water flow is possible.
In addition, solutions for the one-dimensional solute transport in
unsaturated soils during a steady evaporation from the water table will be
presented.
