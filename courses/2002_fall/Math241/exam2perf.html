Section 016-018

Exam 2 Performance

Some comments from your professor:

Exams are an excellent time to assess your standing in the class relative to your personal goals and your peers.

Concerning adjustments in grades: Generally, I will only do this for extraordinary circumstances.  Sometimes I write a tough exam and do not realize it.  Sometimes a question is unintentionally vague or there is a communal misunderstanding.  This is not the case here.  However, I do feel strongly that you should have feedback from your problem sets so that you can study effectively.  The grader has fallen behind (and is trying to catch up) and this put everyone at a disadvantage.  After some thought, I have decided somewhat arbitrarily to adjust every exam score by 5 points.  That is, I think everyone might have earned five points more if they had had their problem sets to study.  This has the cumulative effect of lowering the course grade distribution by 1 point.

Concerning Section 016:  I have never concealed my views on attendence, but I will repeat them here.  This is a medium sized college lecture course.  You may choose to attend lecture or not.  You may choose to attend recitation or not.  Section 016 recitation has distinguished itself by having unusually low attendence, and now, it has distinguished itself by having unusually low performance.  My advice to those who are sleeping through recitation is that it is easier to set the alarm a little earlier than it is to retake a course.  Time is precious.  If you have access to a professor or a teaching assistance, you should make the most of it to listen, ask questions and most of all learn calculus.
