The Problem

Consider the deregulation scenarios depicted in Figure 6 on page 225. Create a mathematical model to determine power output and transmission line capacities along each line. You must assume the following:

  • The local household demand from the three different communities shown in the figure is given, and you have no control over it.

  • You are allowed to vary the power output at each power plant, but the output must be positive because power plants cannot consume large amounts of energy.

  • There is no resistance in the transmission lines as is shown in both the right and left figure.

Your model should be a linear system satisfying all the relevant constraints in the problem. The solution to the linear system should be the power output from each power plant required to keep the grid in balance (``loop flow''). How many equations and how many unknowns are there? Using your model, solve the following problems:

1.
Find a solution analogous to the left figure where there is zero net transfer between the Left and Right Utilities. Your complete solution should include the loads along each power line as well as the output from each power plant. Note: Your solution will be different from the one printed in the article because you are neglecting resistance in the lines.

2.
Find a solution analogous to the right figure where there is 150 MW transfered from Left Utility to Right Utility.

In each, you should investigate if the solution is unique or if there are entire linear spaces of solutions. If the latter is true, how did you choose your particular solution?
