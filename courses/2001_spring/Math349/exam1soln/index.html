7.
[15 pts] If A is an invertible matrix, prove that its inverse is unique. That is, show that there are not two different matrices B and C such that AB = BA = I and AC = CA = I.


Many students struggled with this one.

The proof is simply a matter of algebra. Suppose we had two such inverses.

C = CI = C(AB) = (CA)B = IB = B

Thus C=B and there is only one inverse.
