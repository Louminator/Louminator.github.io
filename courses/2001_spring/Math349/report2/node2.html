The Problem

A course is offered with a maximum enrollment of P students. This enrollment cap cannot be exceeded. You may assume that there are many students available who have the prerequisites for the course, and might be interested in enrolling in the course. Students are attracted to courses with small class sizes. You must calculate the equilibrium probability distribution for the course enrollments. That is, you need to find a vector such that p0 is the probability that no students will enroll in the course, p1 is the probability that one student will enroll in the course, etc. To model the process, you must assume the following.

  • On a given day, if the current enrollment is n, the chance that the course enrollment with rise by k students is


  • On a given day, the chance that enrollment will drop by kstudents is


  • Of course, n can never be less than zero or greater than P.

  • The registration period is long enough to allow the system to reach equilibrium.

Find the equilibrium probability distribution if P=3, P=5 and P = 10.
