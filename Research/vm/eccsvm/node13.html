Concluding Remarks

The use of deforming elliptical Gaussian basis functions for Lagrangian schemes has yielded a naturally adaptive scheme with a high spatial accuracy. More than anything else, the underlying motivation is to boost the accuracy of Lagrangian schemes without sacrificing the compactness of the basis function. ECCSVM achieves this result, but the per element computational cost for the Biot-Savart integration is high. However, it is hard to lose with this approach. Whether one uses direct summation or fast summation, a high spatial order method will eventually outperform any low order scheme.

Though the sample calculations make use of the high order method coupled with adaptive refinement and merging, the merging procedure is omitted in this work in the interests of space and continuity. The methodology is a modification of previous work on merging [23], and more specifics will be provided in a later manuscript.

This introduces numerous avenues for further exploration. For one, this method has only been implemented using direct summation, but elliptical Gaussians are suited for fast summation methods [1, 2, 5, 6, 9, 29]. By decreasing the total number of computational elements and increasing their individual complexity, the method is more suitable for parallelization on MIMD platforms and workstation farms because of the relatively low communication costs. Adding constraints for solid boundaries would be a simple extension, and the elliptical basis functions will resolve boundary layers more efficiently than radially symmetric boundary layers as Marshall, Grant and Teng have found [17, 26]. More challenging still would be extending this method to three dimensions. Though increasing dimensionality adds several new challenging, particularly in evaluating the streamfunction, these problems are accessible using similar asymptotics employed here. Finally, the high order method is a new tool for exploring vorticity dynamics in high Reynolds number flows. The improved spatial resolution and adaptivity make this scheme a natural choice for problems involving vorticity-dominated flows.
