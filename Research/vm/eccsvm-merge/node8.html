Conclusion

 

To be practical, methods relying upon corrected core spreading require merging algorithms to control the implicit exponential growth. This investigation builds a merging algorithm based on error estimates for the merging process. The upper bounds on merger induced errors provide a fast and efficient means of determining a priori error estimates for merging events. The resulting scheme is mathematically robust and does not sacrifice the natural adaptivity of the underlying vortex methods. When implemented for a nontrivial flow, the merging algorithm succeeds in removing extraneous degrees of freedom from the system as shown by the quadratic growth in the problem size over long integration periods.
