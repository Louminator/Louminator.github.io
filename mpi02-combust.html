Double-flame system for stable lean-premixed combustion.

Description.

To to casual visitor:  This site is common place for a group of researchers to work on a report from the Mathematical Problems in Industry workshop held in 2002.  If you do not know why you are here, you probably should not be here, and you will certainly find nothing of value to you here.  Please do not link to this site.

To my project collaborators:  Below, I am posting the latest tar-balls of the report.  Feel free to download them and edit them as much as you would like.  When you arrive at a point where you would like to share your work with everyone, just email me the edited sections and I'll merge them into the next tar-ball.  If you have maple, matlab or mathematica files that you would like to share, I will happily post them as well.
 

Downloading the report tar-ball.

Guidelines for a happy revision process.

     
  1. Please communicate your intentions to everyone else in the group.  It will be painful if two subgroups work on the same section at the same time, and we end up having to merge the contributions.  There is a list of everyone's email addresses in the report.
  2. Please use macros and definitions as much as possible so that it will be easier to set a common notation as we revise the report.
  3. Please look at existing macros and definitions in report.tex before you start revising or writing.  You may save yourself some time and effort.
  4. Do not add macros directly to report.tex.  Instead, just add macros to the top of whichever section you are editing.  I'll move them into the report.tex later when I put together the next tar-ball.
  5. We will be using BibTeX for the report.  (I am assuming that everyone knows how to use it to maintain citations.  If this is not the case, please let me know, and I can post a trackshoe summary of how to use it.)  Please add any references you require to combust.bib.  If I receive a revised combust.bib file, I can easily merge it into the existing one.
